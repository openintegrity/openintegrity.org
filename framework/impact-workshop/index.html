B. Linking impact stories to software best practices

  1. Where does it all start? Impact Stories. (5 min)
  • The goal is to architect the project around people.
  • To create a shared understanding of what drives OII
  • It's to help define the scope and ground the technical conversations.
  • To have traceability about particular technologies, data points, metrics, debates and how they relate to stories.
  • Impact as negative such as "creating some form of harm" or positive as in "making people feel safer or empowered"
  • Impact stories instead of user needs (needs feel more remote than real stories).
  • We had a criteria put together by respected experts but we need to have a more transparent and collaborative process and broaden what is taken into account in the criteria.
  1. Activity (25 min)
  • Think about stories of people's lives being impacted directly by the tool they use.
    • Does it has to be a real or a possible story? (Probably both are fine at this point, given before Snowden. Putting it out there can help validation occur.)
  • Options
    • Wall of post it columns with former OII criteria and ask to write real stories that are a good example of why this matters.
    • Ask to draw the story only with letters, circles and arrows.
    • Each think about a story for 2 minutes and write it on a blue post it note in one sentence "syrian leader skype account being hijacked" or "tunisian blogger arrest"...
      • Tell the story to the 2 other member of the group.
      • Put the blue post it on the wall or on the ground
      • Now write as many yellow post it notes with things that allowed this that were related to the tool or tool maker (taken broadly - including not activating a feature of the tool, or the tool being difficult to use or infrastructure problems...).
      • If you have an important other factor that is not tool related but that you think was also key to enable that impact story then write it in a pink post it note.
  1. Discussion & Conclusion (10 min)
  • Ah ha moments. Discussion. Conclusion. Gap analysis with the former criteria.
